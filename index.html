The Carpathian biosphere reserve

THE MAIN DIRECTIONS OF ACTIVITY

The Carpathian biosphere reserve is a nature-protected scientific-research institution of international significance. The system of ecological monitoring is organized in the reserve, that is a long-term continuous observation over the condition of ecosystems and course of natural processes within them.

DEVELOPMENT PROSPECTS

The Ukrainian Carpathians possess a considerable recreational potential. Despite this, the modern economic-industrial orientation of the region consists in the extensive use of local resources.

FEATURES

The Carpathian mountain system, besides the Alps and the Balkans, is characterized by the largest biological diversity in the European continent. The intensive usage of nature began in the Ukrainian part of the Carpathians later, than in the Western Carpathians.

CARPATHIAN ECOSYSTEMS

More than one thousand species of high vascular plants, 64 species of mammals, 173 species of birds, 9 reptile species, 13 species of amphibian, 23 fish species, more than 10 000 evertebrate animal species etc. are protected in the reserve.
